Dead Watch

It is a simple rule of fiction to note that reality inevitably inspires it, and in return - fiction is reflected in reality. Creatures of fantasy remain relevant in the fiction we consume because they have been designed to reflect real life fears.

Dead Watch is a project that uses vampire fiction as a design tool to capture current events and the societal reaction to them. Vampire fiction and character creation has long reflected the tensions and the view of Eastern Europe from the West. After Bram Stoker reintroduced the vampire to Western audiences with his 1987 novel Dracula, the connection between vampires and Eastern Europe was cemented.

The project explores this connection through repositioning classic texts with examples of modern “othering” of Eastern Europe that were already ongoing at the time of Russia's invasion of Ukraine.

How do/should/must we engage with war?

The final outcomes of this project examine the Western reporting of the invasion through a series of comic books and objects.

Fictionalising current events, by rewriting articles into comic books forces us to question the way in which we
digitally engage in war. These objects are vessels of fiction and represent a captured moment in our current reality by contextualising it within the ongoing turnover of vampire fiction.

I invite you to question when is it appropriate to fictionalise a tragic situation? But where would we be if we didn't?
