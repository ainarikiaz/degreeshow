To Pub or Not to Pub

Through an investigation into the inherent sense of connection and fascination I feel with the British pub, I've created a provocative body of work that looks to enable a space of personal contemplation within the observer. By unpicking the physical, social, and historical fabric of this well-known and loved symbol, my work aims to instigate both internal and external discourses surrounding the role the pub plays in the future of our society. Through the topics contained within the dialogue, I wish to reflect upon personal concerns of mine relating to the abandonment of symbolism within design and men's mental health. The project makes the observer question whether the pub is the last remaining artefact from the past, keeping our society held together, or whether it's the final obstacle we must let go of before we can progress into 'Modern Britain'.

My work is directed towards people who feel a similar sense of attachment to the pub as myself as I believe they are most likely to understand what I'm trying to tap into; however, I believe it has the power to influence a wide range of people in a number of different ways.
